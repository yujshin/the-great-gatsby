THE GREAT GATSBY

CHAPTER 01. A NEW LIFE IN THE EAST

Many years ago, my father gave me some advice, which I have always remembered. " When you are tempted to judge someone, try to remember that many people have not had the some opportunities as yourself."

He didn't elaborate any further. He didn't have to. I knew exactly what he meant. We had an unspoken understanding of one another. Because of his advice, I have always erred on the side of caution. This, in turn, caused me to become known as a non-judgemental sort of character who was then subjected to the mundane conversations of a good number of individuals.I was also unfairly labeled a politician, and whenever I felt that another was going to confine in me, I would often pretend to be asleep or just focus my attentions eleswhere.
